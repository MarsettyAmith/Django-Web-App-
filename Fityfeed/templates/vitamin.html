Vitamin Rich Food Suggestions:

Certainly! Vitamins are essential micronutrients that play a crucial role in maintaining our health. Here are some food sources rich in various vitamins:

Vitamin A:

-Found in beef, liver, eggs, shrimp, fish, fortified milk, sweet potatoes, carrots, pumpkins, spinach, and mangoes1.
-Helps with vision, immune function, and skin health.

Vitamin B (B-complex vitamins):

-B-1 (Thiamine): Present in ham, soymilk, watermelon, and acorn squash.
-B-2 (Riboflavin): Found in milk, yogurt, cheese, whole and enriched grains, and cereals.
-B-3 (Niacin): Available in meat, poultry, fish, fortified and whole grains, mushrooms, and potatoes.
-B-5 (Pantothenic acid): Found in chicken, whole grains, broccoli, avocados, and mushrooms.
-B-6 (Pyridoxine): Present in meat, fish, poultry, legumes, tofu, bananas, and soy products.
-B-7 (Biotin): Available in whole grains, eggs, soybeans, and fish.
-B-9 (Folate): Found in fortified grains and cereals, asparagus, spinach, broccoli, and legumes (black-eyed peas and chickpeas).
-B-12 (Cobalamin): Present in meat, poultry, fish, milk, cheese, fortified soymilk, and cereals.
These vitamins are essential for energy production, nerve function, and red blood cell formation.

Vitamin C:

-Abundant in citrus fruits, potatoes, broccoli, bell peppers, spinach, strawberries, tomatoes, and Brussels sprouts.
-Supports the immune system and acts as an antioxidant1.

Vitamin D:

-Found in fortified milk, cereals, and fatty fish.
-Essential for bone health and immune function.

Vitamin E:

-Present in vegetable oils, leafy green vegetables, whole grains, and nuts.
-Acts as an antioxidant and supports skin health.

Vitamin K:

-Available in cabbage, eggs, milk, spinach, broccoli, and kale.
-Important for blood clotting and bone health
